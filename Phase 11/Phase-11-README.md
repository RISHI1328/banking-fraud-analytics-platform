# Phase 11 – Business Recommendations & Final Deliverables

## Phase Objective
Close the loop: convert the platform's findings into prioritized business recommendations, and package the project for job applications — resume bullets, STAR interview stories, and an executive summary deck.

## Business Objective
Analytics only matters if it changes decisions. This phase demonstrates the BA's final responsibility: translating technical findings into actions leadership can approve, fund, and measure.

## How to use these
1. **The report** goes in the repo as the capstone document — link it prominently from the root README.
2. **Resume bullets:** pick 3–4 per application; keep the project title line with the GitHub link.
3. **STAR stories:** rehearse them out loud; Story 2 (the ETL debugging incident) is the strongest because it's specific and real.
4. **The deck:** attach to the repo and keep a copy ready to screen-share; it's your 5-minute project walkthrough.

## Learning Outcomes
- You can convert analytical findings into prioritized, measurable recommendations.
- You can present a technical project as a business narrative (problem → solution → evidence → action).
- You can package a project for recruiting: quantified bullets, STAR stories, executive deck.

## Interview Questions
1. **What was the business impact of your project?**
   Frame via the recommendations: tiered fraud response, branch-differentiated thresholds, a measurable retention campaign — each tied to a KPI the platform computes.
2. **How do you present technical work to executives?**
   Lead with the problem and the decision needed; keep evidence to a few headline numbers; put method in the appendix. The deck follows exactly that structure.
3. **What are the limitations of your analysis?**
   Synthetic data, a modeled revenue proxy, rules tuned to seeded patterns, and a partial final month — stated openly in the report.
